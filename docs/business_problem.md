# Business Problem

## Context

A multi-region retail company sells products across five geographic regions in the United States.
The business operates across five product categories — Technology, Furniture, Office Supplies,
Clothing, and Sports & Outdoors — and serves three customer segments: Consumer, Corporate, and
Home Office.

As the company has grown, different business teams have struggled to answer fundamental questions
consistently and quickly:

- How is revenue trending month-over-month?
- Which regions are growing fastest, and which are at risk?
- Are discounts helping acquire customers or simply eroding margin?
- Who are the most valuable customers, and when should we expect them to churn?

---

## The Problem

Before this reporting system, the company relied on:

1. **Manual reconciliations** across operational data extracts and spreadsheet reports
2. **Inconsistent business logic** between teams calculating the same KPI differently
3. **Slow reporting cycles** in which executive reporting arrived after decisions had already been made

This created three failure modes:

| Failure | Business Impact |
|---|---|
| Inconsistent metric definitions | Different teams reporting different revenue or margin values for the same period |
| No single source of truth | Repeated reconciliation effort before leadership meetings |
| Slow reporting cycle | Delayed visibility into regional performance and margin pressure |

---

## Business Questions This Reporting System Answers

• Which regions contribute the highest revenue?

• Which products consistently generate the highest sales?

• How does monthly revenue change over time?

• Which customers contribute the greatest lifetime value?

• Which categories are underperforming?

• Are there data quality issues affecting reporting?

---

### Expected Reporting Outcomes

• Standardized business metrics across reporting.

• Reproducible SQL-based reporting workflow.

• Executive dashboards built from governed analytical datasets.

• Improved visibility into regional, product, and customer performance.

• Reusable serverless reporting architecture using AWS services.
